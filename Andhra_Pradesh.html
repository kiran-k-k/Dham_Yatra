6 Famous Temples of Andhra Pradesh

Tirumala Venkateswara Temple.

Tirupati.

The Tirumala Venkateswara Temple, also known as the Balaji Temple, is a major pilgrimage site dedicated to Lord Vishnu in the age of Kali. Located in the scenic hills of Tirumala, it is considered an earthly manifestation of Vaikuntam and is one of India's most visited temples.

Yaganti Temple.

Nandyal.

aganti Temple in Andhra Pradesh's Kurnool district is a revered shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva, notably in the form of Ardhanarishvara. Believed to have manifested from sage Agastya's penance, the temple is set amidst scenic hills, creating a tranquil atmosphere.

Ahobilam Temple.

Kurnool.

Ahobilam Temple, located in the Eastern Ghats of Andhra Pradesh, is a revered site dedicated to Lord Narasimha, an incarnation of Vishnu. The temple complex features Upper and Lower Ahobilam shrines and nine sacred sites linked to Hindu mythology.

Simhachalam Temple.

Visakhapatnam.

Simhachalam Temple in Visakhapatnam, Andhra Pradesh, is a prominent Hindu shrine dedicated to Lord Narasimha, an avatar of Vishnu. Perched on Simhachalam Hills, it offers stunning views and features a blend of Dravidian and Kalinga architectural styles with intricate carvings and vibrant sculptures.

Kanaka Durga Temple.

Vijayawada.

The Kanaka Durga Temple in Vijayawada, Andhra Pradesh, is a major Hindu pilgrimage site dedicated to Goddess Kanaka Durga. Perched on Indrakeeladri Hill overlooking the Krishna River, the temple is renowned for its intricate architecture and vibrant decorations.

Mangalagiri Temple.

Mangalagiri.

Mangalagiri Temple in Andhra Pradesh is a significant Hindu shrine dedicated to Lord Narasimha, an incarnation of Vishnu. Located on "Auspicious Hill," the temple is revered for its spiritual ambiance and architectural beauty. It attracts devotees nationwide, who visit for blessings and wish fulfillment.
