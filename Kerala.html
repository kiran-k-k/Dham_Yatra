6 Famous Temples of Kerala

Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple.

Thiruvananthapuram.

The Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple in Thiruvananthapuram, Kerala, is a renowned 8th-century shrine dedicated to Lord Vishnu. Built in the Dravidian style of architecture, this temple is one of the 108 Divya Desams, the sacred Vishnu temples.

Sabarimala Sastha Temple.

Pathanamthitta.

The Sabarimala Sastha Temple, located near the Periyar Tiger Reserve in Kerala, is one of the most popular pilgrimage sites in the state. Dedicated to Lord Ayyappan, it attracts around 50 million pilgrims annually.

Attukal Bhagavathy Temple.

Thiruvananthapuram.

Just 2 kilometers from the Sree Padmanabhaswamy Temple, the Attukal Bhagavathy Temple is another renowned place of worship in Kerala. Dedicated to Goddess Kannaki, an incarnation of Goddess Parvati and considered the Supreme Mother, the temple is a significant spiritual site.

Ambalapuzha Sree Krishna Temple.

Ambalapuzha.

The Ambalapuzha Sree Krishna Temple, built in the 17th century, is dedicated to Lord Krishna in his child form, Unni Krishna. A beautiful legend associated with the temple leads to the offering of Palpayasam, a delicious sweet pudding, to devotees.

Chottanikkara Temple.

Chottanikkara.

The Chottanikkara Temple is one of Kerala's most renowned temples, celebrated for its architectural beauty. The presiding deity, Chottanikkara Devi, is worshipped in three forms throughout the day: as Saraswati in the morning, Lakshmi at noon, and Durga in the evening.

Vadakkunnathan Temple.

Thrissur.

Vadakkunnathan Temple, a National Monument under the AMASR Act, is a revered Shiva temple in Kerala. Believed to be the first temple built by Parasuram, the sixth incarnation of Vishnu, it features exquisite mural paintings and monumental towers.
