6 Famous Temples of Karnataka

Virupaksha Temple.

Hampi.

Virupaksha Temple in Karnataka, a prominent Hindu pilgrimage site, is a remarkable remnant of the ancient Vijayanagar Empire. Known for its exquisite architecture and murals, the temple features unique elements like a three-headed Nandi idol and a gopuram that casts an inverted shadow through a pinhole camera effect.

Hoysaleswara Temple.

Halebidur.

The 900-year-old Hoysaleswara Temple stands as a timeless storyteller, its intricate carvings illuminated by the morning sun. Built in the 12th century over 30 years, this Dravidian masterpiece, dedicated to Lord Shiva, features a grand Mandapa and sanctum sanctorum.

Sri Sharadamba Temple.

Sringeri.

The Sri Sharadamba Temple in Sringeri, Karnataka, is a revered Hindu pilgrimage site founded by the philosopher Adi Shankaracharya. Dedicated to Goddess Sharadamba, the deity of wisdom, learning, music, and arts, the temple houses a golden statue of the goddess. Originally, a sandalwood idol was installed by Adi Shankaracharya, later replaced by the current golden icon.

Chamundeshwari Temple.

Mysuru.

Perched atop Chamundi Hills in Mysuru, Karnataka, the Chamundeshwari Temple is a majestic symbol of Hindu faith and Dravidian architecture. Dedicated to Goddess Chamundeshwari, an incarnation of Parvati, the temple stands at 3,489 feet, offering stunning views of Mysore, including the Lalitha Mahal Palace.

Murdeshwar Temple.

Murdeshwar.

The Murudeshwar Temple in Karnataka is a renowned pilgrimage site dedicated to Lord Shiva, known as Sri Mridesa Linga. Situated atop Kanduka Hills and surrounded by the Arabian Sea, the temple is famous for its 37-meter-tall Shiva statue, the second tallest in the world, and its 75-meter Raj Gopuram.
